NPK (Nitrogen-Phosphorus-Potassium) is fundamental in plant growth, nutrition and development.

Nitrogen:

  • Major component of amino acids, proteins, and chlorophyll,

  • Essential for cell growth, energy transfer, and photosynthesis.

  • Adequate nitrogen promotes vigorous foliage development and overall plant growth.

    Phosphorus:

  • Crucial for energy transfer and storage in the form of ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which drives many plant processes.

  • Promotes strong root growth, flowering, and fruiting and is essential for the formation of DNA, RNA, and nucleic acids.

    Potassium:

  • Helps in regulating water uptake and loss by controlling stomatal openings, which affects transpiration and water use efficiency.

  • Activates enzymes involved in photosynthesis, respiration, and protein synthesis

  • Enhances the plant’s ability to resist diseases and pests and improves overall plant health.
